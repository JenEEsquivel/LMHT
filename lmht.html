English

Contextual Aid

The body is between the head and foot. At some point in the history of the English lexicon, it became idiomatic to refer to the main portion of a writer's work as his body of work. The first few chapters, as an introduction, are the beginning, or head. The last few chapters are the end, or foot. The inbetween is the main content; therefore, the body.
